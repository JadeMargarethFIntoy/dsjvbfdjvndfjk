The water cycle is a biochemical cycle that involve the continuous movement of water on, above and below the surface of the earth.
The mass of water on earth remains fairly constant overtime. However the paritioning of the water into the major reservoirs of ice,
fresh water, salt water, and atmospheric water is variable and depends on climatic variables.

Evaporation is a type of vaporization that occurs on the surface of a liquid as it changes into the gas phase.
Condensation is the change of the state of matter from the gas phase into the liquid phase, and it cools the
water vapor into the atmosphere to form clouds. Precipitation occurs when a portion of the atmosphere becomes
saturated with water vapor reaching 100% relative humidity so that the water condenses and precipitates or falls.
Infiltration is the process by which water on the ground surface enters the soil.
Runoff is the unconfined flow of water over the ground surface.
